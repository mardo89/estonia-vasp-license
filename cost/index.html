The own-funds requirement — Estonia's biggest cost driver

Estonia's own-funds requirement is unique in the EU. On top of €100,000 paid-up share capital, Estonian VASPs/CASPs must maintain regulatory own funds based on the activity:

Activity scopeRequired own funds
Light VASP — exchange of crypto for fiat (no custody)€125,000
Standard VASP — exchange + transfer services€175,000–€250,000
Full VASP — exchange + custody + wallet provider€350,000

Critical: Own funds are working capital you can use, but you cannot fall below the minimum. RAB monitors capital ratios quarterly. Falling below triggers supervision actions including possible suspension of authorization.

Why Estonia substance is so expensive vs Lithuania

Estonia's substance requirements were tightened significantly in 2022-2024 after several high-profile VASP failures. The bar is now:

  • Local Estonian-resident director mandatory (€8K-$30K/year for fractional or part-time)
  • Real Tallinn office mandatory (no virtual address) — €3K-$15K/year for office + utilities
  • Estonia-based MLRO with relevant credentials (CAMS, ICA Diploma) — €5K-$15K/year fractional
  • RAB inspections can be conducted at the office — must be inspection-ready
  • RAB approval of directors and UBOs — fit-and-proper review of every key person

Combined: $16K-$60K/year in mandatory substance costs vs Lithuania's ~$15K-$50K (Lithuania substance is "expected" but more flexible — fractional MLRO often acceptable, no mandatory physical office).

What you get for the higher cost

Estonia's premium isn't arbitrary. The substance + capital requirements buy:

  1. Pioneer-jurisdiction credibility. Estonia issued the world's first crypto licenses in 2017. Some institutional clients value this history.
  2. 0% retained-earnings tax. Estonia uniquely doesn't tax corporate profits until distributed (22% on dividends). For crypto businesses reinvesting profits, capital can compound without annual tax drag.
  3. Strict regulatory regime. RAB's tightened scrutiny since 2022 means surviving Estonian VASPs are higher-quality operators — bank acceptance is correspondingly easier.
  4. Local Estonian advisor (us). X24Consulting OÜ is itself an Estonian Tallinn-based company. Direct RAB relationships, no foreign-intermediary friction.

FAQ — Estonia VASP/CASP cost

What's the total cost of an Estonia VASP/CASP license in 2026?

Year-1 setup: $30,000-$70,000 USD (excluding capital). Includes €10,000 RAB application fee, €15,000-€30,000 AML/KYC program, $10,000-$30,000 local director + Tallinn office substance, $5,000-$15,000 Estonian legal counsel. PLUS €100,000 paid-up capital and €125,000-€350,000 own funds (held throughout operations, activity-dependent). Annual maintenance: $20,000-$40,000.

How much capital does an Estonian VASP need to lock up?

€100,000 paid-up share capital PLUS €125,000-€350,000 own funds depending on activity scope. Light activities (advisory, basic exchange) require €125K own funds; full exchange + custody requires €350K. Capital is held — not consumed — but cannot be deployed elsewhere. Total locked capital: €225,000-€450,000+.

Why is Estonia VASP more expensive than Lithuania or Panama?

Estonia raised substance requirements significantly in 2022-2024: mandatory local Estonian-resident director, mandatory physical Tallinn office (no virtual address), RAB approval of all directors and UBOs, higher own-funds requirements (€125K-€350K vs Lithuania's standard €125K). The substance + capital combination makes Estonia ~2-3x more expensive than Lithuania despite similar EU MiCA passporting outcome.

Is Estonia worth the premium over Lithuania?

For most operators: no. Lithuania CASP delivers the same EU MiCA passport at ~70% of total economic cost with significantly lighter substance requirements. Estonia wins only when you have genuine Estonian presence (founder, team, office) or specifically need pioneer-jurisdiction credibility for institutional clients. We'll honestly recommend Lithuania to most applicants if Estonia substance can't be committed.

What's Estonia's tax structure for crypto companies?

Estonia uniquely does not tax retained corporate profits — only distributed profits are taxed (22% on dividends since 2025). For crypto businesses reinvesting profits, capital can compound without annual tax drag. For operators distributing profits regularly, Lithuania's 15% (or 5% small entity) often beats Estonia's 22% on distribution. Estonia also has 0% capital gains tax for individuals on crypto holdings (held >1 year).
