Estonia VASP/CASP Requirements 2026: Full RAB Checklist

Estonia tightened crypto-licensing requirements significantly in 2022-2024. Today's bar: real Estonian substance (local director + Tallinn office + Estonian MLRO), significant capital (€225K-€450K+), and comprehensive AML compliance. Here's the complete RAB requirements checklist.

Capital requirements — €225K-€450K+ locked

Estonia has the highest capital requirement of any major EU crypto jurisdiction. Two layers:

1. Paid-up share capital — €100,000

Estonian OÜ formation requires only €2,500 share capital, but VASP/CASP authorization mandates €100,000 paid-up. This must be deposited in the OÜ's Estonian bank account (LHV, SEB, Citadele, etc.) before submitting the RAB application. Auditor confirmation required.

2. Own funds — €125,000–€350,000 depending on activity

On top of share capital, Estonian VASPs/CASPs must maintain regulatory own funds:

Activity scopeRequired own fundsTotal locked capital
Light VASP — crypto-fiat exchange only, no custody€125,000€225,000
Standard VASP — exchange + transfer services€175,000–€250,000€275,000–€350,000
Full VASP — exchange + custody + wallet provider€350,000€450,000

Own funds remain working capital — you can use them for operations — but you cannot fall below the minimum. RAB monitors capital ratios quarterly. Falling below triggers supervision actions.

Substance requirements — real Estonian presence

Local Estonian-resident director (mandatory since 2022)

At least 1 board member must be Estonian resident or have meaningful Estonian presence. Key facts:

  • Cannot be a virtual or paper-only role
  • RAB conducts fit-and-proper review on all directors
  • Director must be available for RAB inspections + business operations
  • Typical cost: $8,000-$30,000/year for fractional or part-time arrangement
  • X24Consulting OÜ can introduce vetted Estonian directors

Physical Tallinn office (mandatory since 2023)

RAB requires a real physical office in Estonia. Key facts:

  • Virtual office addresses are rejected
  • RAB inspections can be conducted at the office
  • Must be inspection-ready (workspace, document access, etc.)
  • Typical cost: $3,000-$15,000/year for office + utilities
  • Most VASPs use shared offices in Tallinn business districts

Estonia-based MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer)

The MLRO must be physically based in or regularly present in Estonia. Key facts:

  • Recognized AML credential required (CAMS, ICA Diploma in AML, or equivalent)
  • Independent of operational management — cannot be the CEO
  • Available for RAB queries and examinations
  • Typical cost: $5,000-$15,000/year fractional, $40,000-$80,000/year full-time

RAB fit-and-proper review

RAB conducts fit-and-proper review on:

  • All board members (directors)
  • The MLRO
  • The IT/cybersecurity officer
  • Any individuals owning ≥10% of voting rights
  • Any individuals exercising effective control

Fit-and-proper criteria include:

  1. Reputation — no criminal convictions for financial crimes; no disciplinary findings by financial regulators
  2. Knowledge and experience — relevant financial-services or crypto experience (5+ years for board, 3+ for MLRO)
  3. Time commitment — sufficient availability for the role
  4. Conflicts of interest — disclosure of all relevant connections, related-party transactions

Each key person submits ~15-20 page fit-and-proper questionnaire + supporting documents (CV, criminal record check, professional references, signed declarations).

Documents required (per key person)

RAB fit-and-proper documentation

  1. Detailed CV — full work history with crypto/financial-services emphasis
  2. Criminal record check — apostilled, current within 3 months, from country of residence
  3. 2 professional references — from lawyer, accountant, or notary on letterhead
  4. Notarized passport copy — apostilled if non-Estonian
  5. Proof of address — utility bill or bank statement <3 months
  6. UBO declaration — for ≥10% ownership
  7. Source-of-funds documentation — for capital deposit
  8. Signed fit-and-proper questionnaire — RAB-provided form

AML/CFT compliance program — 5AMLD/6AMLD aligned

Estonia's AML framework follows EU 5AMLD/6AMLD. RAB requires comprehensive program:

  • Customer Due Diligence (CDD/EDD) — ID verification, source-of-funds, PEP/sanctions screening
  • Risk assessment — documented analysis of ML/TF risks specific to YOUR business
  • Transaction monitoring — automated where volumes justify, manual where not
  • Reporting to FIU — Suspicious Activity Reports to Estonia's Financial Intelligence Unit (FCIS-equivalent)
  • Record-keeping — 5 years (EU minimum)
  • Annual training — for all staff with documented attendance
  • Independent audit — annual review of the AML program
  • MLRO appointment — named, fit-and-proper, Estonia-based

IT and operational requirements

  • Cybersecurity framework — ISO 27001 alignment expected
  • Custody segregation — customer crypto-assets segregated from company funds (on-chain proof)
  • Business continuity plan — documented procedures, annually tested
  • Outsourcing arrangements — written contracts with all critical service providers
  • Data protection — GDPR-compliant data handling

Eligibility — who can apply

You CAN apply if:

  • You can commit Estonian substance (local director + Tallinn office + Estonian MLRO)
  • You can lock €225,000-€450,000+ capital throughout operations
  • You and key personnel pass RAB fit-and-proper review
  • You have legitimate source of funds for capital deposit
  • Your business model is legal and aligned with Estonian/EU law

You CANNOT apply if:

  • You're unable to commit local Estonian substance — Lithuania CASP is better fit
  • You can't lock €225K+ capital — Panama (€0) or Canada MSB ($0) is better fit
  • Key personnel have undisclosed criminal convictions for financial crimes
  • You appear on UN, EU, or major sanctions lists
  • Your business model is incompatible with EU AML/CFT framework

FAQ — Estonia VASP/CASP requirements

What capital does Estonia VASP/CASP require?

€100,000 minimum paid-up share capital for the Estonian OÜ, PLUS €125,000-€350,000 own funds (regulatory reserve) depending on activity scope. Light VASP (exchange only): €125K own funds. Standard VASP (exchange + transfer): €175K-€250K. Full VASP (exchange + custody + wallet): €350K. Total locked capital: €225,000-€450,000.

Does Estonia require a local Estonian director?

Yes — mandatory since 2022. At least 1 board member must be Estonian resident or have meaningful Estonian presence. Cannot be a virtual or paper-only role. RAB conducts fit-and-proper review on all directors. Cost typically $8,000-$30,000/year for a fractional or part-time arrangement.

Is a physical Estonian office required?

Yes — mandatory since 2023. RAB requires a real physical office in Estonia (typically Tallinn). Virtual office addresses are rejected. RAB inspections can be conducted at the office, so it must be inspection-ready. Typical cost: $3,000-$15,000/year for office + utilities + receptionist coverage.

What documents do directors and UBOs need to provide?

Per key person: detailed CV, criminal record check (apostilled, <3 months old), 2 professional references on letterhead, notarized passport copy, proof of address (utility bill or bank statement <3 months), UBO declaration for ≥10% ownership, source-of-funds documentation, signed RAB fit-and-proper questionnaire. ~15-20 page questionnaire per person.

Can a foreign-incorporated entity apply for Estonian VASP/CASP?

No. Estonian VASP/CASP authorization requires an Estonian OÜ (Osaühing — private limited company). Foreign branches or subsidiaries are not accepted. Foreign-headquartered groups typically structure as: Foreign Parent → Estonian OÜ (VASP/CASP).
